Unmanned System for Fetching and Shelving Drugs in Pharmaceutical Facilities (On progress)

This project aims to engineer a fully autonomous robotic arm specifically designed for pharmaceutical environments, tasked with the accurate shelving and retrieval of medication boxes utilizing visual data inputs, such as stereo vision, coupled with robust dexterous manipulation techniques. On-Going Work

RAVEN (On progress)

This project is driven by the challenges faced in stabilizing drones within complex aerodynamic conditions during rescue operations. It employs an Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) on the flight controller to ascertain the drone's Cartesian position. While a conventional PID controller is currently used, transitioning to a Neural Network approach could potentially yield better stabilization results. To further this development, a controlled environment is being constructed entirely from scratch, equipped with infrared tracking cameras. This setup is designed to simulate complex aerodynamic scenarios and capture the 3D position of the drone, allowing for precise comparison with IMU data. (Project Lead)

Risk factor identification and classification of malnutrition among under-five children in Bangladesh: Machine learning and statistical approach,
Tasfin Mahmud, Tayab Uddin Wara, Chironjeet Das Joy,
Heliyon, (Under Review)

This study examines factors contributing to malnutrition in under-five children using data from the MICS-2019 survey, employing Decision Tree, Random Forest, Support Vector Machine, and Multi-layer Perceptron (MLP) models. Random Forest and MLP showed excellent performance, with accuracies of 98.55% and 98.69%, respectively. Pearson correlation analysis revealed key factors like Weight for Age Z Score (WAZ2), Weight for Height Z Score (WHZ2), and breastfeeding status as significant contributors to malnutrition. The findings provide valuable insights into the classification and prediction of malnutrition stages.

The Flying Raijin

(Independent project)

Named after the Fourth Hokage's teleportation technique in "Naruto," the Flying Raijin is a custom-built 5-inch FPV drone engineered for exceptional speed, accelerating from 0 to 150 km/h in just 2 seconds, as verified by the onboard GPS module. The drone utilizes an STM32 F411 MCU and operates on a 16V system. It is equipped with a high-resolution camera, a low-latency video transmitter, and multiple onboard sensors, all of which enhance the system's overall performance and capabilities.
